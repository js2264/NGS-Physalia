Program

Classes are from:

  • 2 to 8 pm Paris time.
  • 1 to 7 pm London time.
  • 8 am to 2 pm NY time.
  • 5 am to 11 am SF time.

Day 1 - Next-generation sequencing data processing

Lectures

  • Epigenomics introduction
  • General processing of NGS data
  • Managing worfklows: bash, Snakemake, Nextflow, …

Labs

  • Step-by-step processing of ChIP-seq data

Day 2 - ChIP-seq analysis

Lectures

  • Identifying DNA-binding protein targets
  • R/Bioconductor 101: Data import, manipulating genomic ranges, …

Labs

  • Identification of TF-binding site or epigenomic histone modification using ChIP-seq peak calling
  • Identification of motifs and motif enrichment analysis

Day 3 - RNA-seq analysis

Lectures

  • Estimating transcript abundance from RNA-seq
  • R/Bioconductor 201: SummarizedExperiment

Labs

  • Differential gene expression analysis between samples

Day 4 - ATAC-seq

Lectures

  • Peak callers: ChIP-seq vs. ATAC-seq
  • R/Bioconductor 301: Databases, resources, …

Labs

  • Identification of ATAC-seq peaks on multiple samples using YAPC
  • Differential binding analysis and clustering of ATAC-seq peaks
  • Study of chromatin accessibility at promoters with V-plots

Day 5 - Integration and visualization of different omics data

Lectures

  • Regulatory states from histone marks
  • Identification of transcription factor target genes using RNA-seq and ChIP-seq

Labs

  • Crossing results from ATAC-seq with chromHMM
  • Integration of ChIP-seq data and RNA-seq data
  • Analysis with external databases: GO analysis, protein functional interactions, …
